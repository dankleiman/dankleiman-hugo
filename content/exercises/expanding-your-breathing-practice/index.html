Straw Breathing


Straw breathing trains your diaphragm to be stronger, because you practice more forceful exhales. Without moving into strain, you will play with the duration and concentration of your exhale, using your mouth instead of your nose. Combined with a shorter inhale through the nose, this is a much more challenging exercise.

Twisting to Open the Ribs


Adding twisting movements that favor one side of the body will highlight proper rib movement and help you expand your breath into a new area. The focus of this exercise is on the side-ways expansion of the rib cage. Before you attempt it in your practice flow, check out the instructional video and practice the movements first. Later, coordinate the breathing with the movements.
