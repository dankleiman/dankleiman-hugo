It can have several different rates--the easiest to find is about a one and two and three and four beat, but there is a longer one that can be more than 10 seconds. One of the easier ways to feel the pulse of the wave is to feel how two of the major bones of the skull--the occipital bone at the back and the sphenoid bone in the front work as a hinge. As the spine rises, these two hinge towards each other and draw fluid up into the ventricles of the brain; as they release and fall down, they release fluid down towards the pelvis. Worth trying. Learned how to feel these rhythms in a workshop on craniosacral therapy.
